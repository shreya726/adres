What is ADRES?

Automated Dual Route Error Scoring (ADRES) is a tool for analyzing reading and writing errors at the single-word level. ADRES was designed at the Boston University Aphasia Research Laboratory to assess print processing for individuals with the acquired language deficits known as alexia and agraphia. With this system, clinicians and researchers, alike, can gain a more thorough understanding of their clients’ language patterns.

Users input the target (i.e., the word your client attempted to read/write) and the response (i.e., their actual production). Then ADRES returns a binary score – Sublexical, Lexical – based on the response’s accuracy in both spelling and meaning. Scores range from 0 – 9 in the sublexical and lexical systems, with 0 indicating “no response” and 9 indicating a “correct” production.

Why use ADRES?

By evaluating results in this way, it is possible to:
  • Better understand the function and dysfunction of clients’ print processing systems
  • Determine what aspect(s) of the language system might be appropriate to target in treatment
  • Quantify progress beyond the level of “correct” vs. “incorrect” scores

Semantic Scoring

In the semantic version of ADRES, accurate descriptions/gestures earn a higher score than unrelated words, and inaccurate descriptions/gestures earn a higher score than novel nonwords.
